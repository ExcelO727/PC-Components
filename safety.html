Safety

Electrostatic Discharge

Electrostatic discharge is when static electricity builds up on one surface, putting it off balance, then it transfers onto another surface to balance out the charge causing a shock that is able to fry PC components if you do not take the proper procedures to protect against ESD.

In order for humans the feel the classic shock that you feel when you touch and inbalanced metal object, there needs to be at least 1500 volts of ESD, but this number is much lower for PC components, being less than 10 volts before a PC component can get fried. This is why it is so dangerous to work on PC components without proper antistatic gear, since a components could get fried and we would not even feel it.

How to protect against ESD

There are 4 ways to protect against ESD when working with PC components.

  1. Antistatic Wristbands
  2. Antistatic Workbenches
  3. Antistatic Bags
  4. Antistatic Mats
Antistatic Wristbands

Antistatic wristbands are straps that you put on your hand before working inside a PC or with PC components. There is a clip at the end of the wristband that has to attach to a part of the PC case that is not painted. This lets it take the static electricity that could generate in the PC and put it into your hand where it can not harm you.
